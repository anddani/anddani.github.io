The One Cup is probably the most iconic convenience store sake in Japan, and for good reason. It's unpretentious and remarkably consistent.

Poured straight from the little glass cup it comes in, you get a clean, light-bodied sake with no real surprises. The rice character is straightforward — no frills, no pretense. A faint sweetness on the finish keeps it from being too austere.

Not something you'd open for a special occasion, but exactly right for a quick sip on the go or paired with simple bar snacks. At its price point it's hard to argue with.
